# Glossary

<QueryInterface />

This glossary provides definitions for key terms and concepts used throughout the AI/Spec-Driven Book on Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics.

## A

**Action** - A ROS 2 communication pattern for long-running tasks with feedback and goal management.

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)** - The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems, particularly in robotics applications.

**Autonomous Movement** - The ability of a robot to navigate and perform tasks without human intervention.

## B

**Behavior Tree** - A hierarchical model used in robotics and AI to structure the execution of actions and decisions.

## C

**Capstone Project** - A comprehensive project that integrates knowledge from all modules to demonstrate complete system implementation.

**Cognitive Planning** - The process of creating action plans based on understanding and reasoning about the environment and goals.

## D

**Deep Learning** - A subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with representation learning.

**Digital Twin** - A virtual replica of a physical system used for simulation, testing, and analysis.

## E

**Embodied Intelligence** - Intelligence that emerges from the interaction between an agent and its physical environment.

**Exercise** - A hands-on activity designed to reinforce learning objectives and provide practical experience.

## F

**Forward Kinematics** - The use of joint parameters to compute the position and orientation of the end-effector.

## G

**Gazebo** - A 3D simulation environment for robotics that provides accurate physics simulation.

## H

**Humanoid Robot** - A robot with a human-like body structure, typically having a head, torso, two arms, and two legs.

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)** - The study of interactions between humans and robots.

## I

**Inverse Kinematics** - The process of calculating joint parameters required to place the end-effector at a desired position.

**Isaac Sim** - NVIDIA's next-generation simulation environment for robotics development.

**Isaac ROS** - NVIDIA's hardware-accelerated robot software stack.

## K

**Kinematics** - The study of motion without considering the forces that cause the motion.

## L

**Learning Objectives** - Specific, measurable outcomes that describe what students will be able to do after completing a module.

**Locomotion** - The ability to move from one place to another, particularly relevant for bipedal robots.

## M

**Module** - A structured learning unit covering specific robotics concepts, containing learning objectives, content, exercises, and checkpoints.

**Middleware** - Software that provides common services and capabilities to applications beyond what's offered by the operating system.

## N

**Navigation** - The process by which a robot determines and follows a path from its current location to a desired destination.

**Node** - A process that performs computation in ROS 2.

## P

**Perception Pipeline** - A sequence of processing steps that transform sensor data into meaningful information about the environment.

**Physical AI** - The intersection of artificial intelligence and physical systems, particularly robots.

**Python Agent** - A program written in Python that interfaces with ROS controllers.

## R

**Reinforcement Learning** - A type of machine learning where an agent learns to make decisions by performing actions and receiving rewards.

**Robot Operating System (ROS)** - A flexible framework for writing robot software that provides hardware abstraction and device drivers.

**ROS 2** - The second generation of the Robot Operating System with improved security, real-time capabilities, and multi-robot support.

**ROS Controller** - Software components that manage the interface between ROS and robot hardware.

## S

**Service** - A ROS 2 communication pattern for request-response interactions.

**Simulation** - The imitation of the operation of a real-world process or system over time.

**SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)** - The computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it.

**VSLAM (Visual SLAM)** - SLAM using visual sensors like cameras.

## T

**Topic** - A ROS 2 communication pattern for publish-subscribe messaging.

## U

**URDF (Unified Robot Description Format)** - An XML format for representing a robot model.

**Unity** - A real-time 3D development platform used for visualization and simulation in robotics.

## V

**Vision-Language-Action (VLA)** - Systems that process visual input, understand language commands, and execute corresponding robotic actions.

**VLA System** - A system that integrates vision, language understanding, and robotic action execution.

## W

**Whisper** - OpenAI's automatic speech recognition system used for voice-to-text conversion.

## X, Y, Z

**Zero-shot Learning** - The ability to perform tasks without prior training on that specific task.

---

*This glossary is designed to support your learning throughout the course. Terms are defined in the context of robotics and AI applications as used in this educational material.*
